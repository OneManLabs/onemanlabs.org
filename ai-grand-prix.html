Playground

A practice simulator for autonomous drone racing.

A deterministic environment where teams can iterate on perception, planning, and control code against a realistic racing stack.

Autopilot

03A Perception, planning, control, and flight dynamics in a repeatable loop.

01 Why it exists

Train against the whole flight stack.

Autonomous racing is more than a vision model. Camera timing, coordinate frames, flight dynamics, controller behavior, course geometry, and solver output all need to agree. The Playground makes that complete loop available in one reproducible environment.

It combines Elodin physics and rendering with a real Betaflight SITL controller and a focused solver interface so experimentation stays close to the competition problem.

02 Inside the simulator
01

6-DOF physics

Deterministic flight dynamics model a generic five-inch racing quad at multiple sensor rates.

02

Real flight control

Betaflight SITL runs in lockstep with the simulation and communicates through standard control paths.

03

Competition camera

A forward FPV camera matches published image dimensions, intrinsics, rate, and up-tilt.

04

Focused solver API

Competitors can concentrate on one autopilot contract instead of rebuilding simulation infrastructure.

05

Measured courses

Gate geometry, pass timing, race state, and lap results are tracked automatically.

06

Inspectable runs

Telemetry can be exported to CSV and FPV streams to video for replay, regression, and offline analysis.

Open source

Built for teams to use now.

The Playground is published under Apache 2.0 and developed in the open with architecture notes, a solver contract, tests, and a cross-platform setup path.
